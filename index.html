Financial Management 1 (4 Credits)

​In Financial Management 1, students receive an introduction to Financial and Managerial Accounting, learning how to prepare and assess financial information to understand the financial impact of business decisions. The module is divided into two parts. The first part covers the basics of accounting, including transaction analysis, the adjusting process, and the preparation of key financial statements like the Income Statement and Balance Sheet. Students explore the accounting system behind these statements, focusing on the double-entry accounting system used globally. The second part introduces managerial accounting, with emphasis on cost concepts essential for planning and business decisions. This includes performing cost-volume-profit analyses and preparing operating and financial budgets. While this module does not train students to become accountants, it equips them with fundamental skills for interpreting financial information and understanding business operations from a financial perspective.

SAP-ERP (4 Credits)

An Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) system can be thought of as a company-wide information system that tightly integrates all aspects of a business. ERP systems are highly integrated, enterprise-wide software packages that automate core corporate activities - business processes - such as manufacturing, supply and distribution, financials and human resources. ERP systems link all areas of a business with external suppliers and customers into a tightly integrated system with shared data and visibility. ERP systems have a major impact on the processes in an organisation. Many companies are implementing ERP systems, and (future) employees must become familiar with new process-oriented working methods. Higher-educated staff must have an overview of various complex automated processes in their area of competence, for example in the areas of finance or logistics. To facilitate this, students are trained in the ERP system SAP. SAP is a worldwide leader in ERP systems.
