Financial Management 1 (4 Credits)

Financial Management 1 introduces students to Financial and Managerial Accounting, split into two parts. The first part addresses the basics of accounting, including transaction analysis, the adjusting process, and preparation of financial statements like the Income Statement and Balance Sheet. It emphasizes the global double-entry accounting system. The second part focuses on managerial accounting, highlighting essential cost concepts, cost-volume-profit analysis, and budget preparation. Although the module doesn't prepare students to become accountants, it equips them with fundamental skills for interpreting financial information and understanding business operations financially.

SAP-ERP (4 Credits)

An Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) system integrates all facets of a business into a cohesive information system that spans the entire organization. ERP systems are comprehensive, highly integrated software packages designed to automate essential business processes such as manufacturing, supply chain management, finance, and human resources. These systems connect various business areas with external stakeholders—suppliers and customers—into a unified system with shared data and visibility, significantly impacting organizational processes. As many companies adopt ERP systems, it becomes imperative for (future) employees to adapt to new process-oriented working methods. Staff with higher education are expected to understand various complex automated processes within their fields, such as finance or logistics. To support this requirement, students receive training in the ERP system SAP, a global leader in ERP solutions.

Transnational Commercial Law (3 Credits)

Law is essential for managing business risks, regulating behavior, preventing conflicts, and resolving disputes. For managers, legal knowledge is key to making informed decisions, enhancing negotiation positions, and leveraging legal opportunities while avoiding risks. **Topics covered include:** **European Union Law:** - EU Integration: History, impact on sovereignty. - EU Institutions: Free Movement of Goods & Persons, landmark cases. - EU Competition Law: Cartels, abuse of dominance, merger control. **Transnational Commercial Law:** - Contract law, applicable law, dispute resolution (litigation, arbitration). - Uniform rules: INCOTERMS, CISG, Hague-(Visby) Rules. - Export instruments: Bills of Lading, Letters of Credit. - Intellectual property rights, global business forms. - International trade frameworks: WTO, GATT, legal systems. - Current legal and geopolitical trade issues.
